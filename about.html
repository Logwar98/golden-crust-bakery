Timeline

2012

The Golden Crust Bakery opened its doors in Gisborne, Founded by 2 people Sarah and Tom Smith, the bakery quickly became known for its warm, inviting atmosphere and the irresistible smell of freshly baked bread. Their commitment to using locally sourced ingredients resonated with the locals, making it a beloved spot for breakfast and lunch.

2015

By 2015, the Golden Crust Bakery had expanded its offerings to include a variety of specialty cakes and pastries. They introduced sandwiches and meats, catering to the lunchtime crowd. This year also marked their first participation in the Gisborne Food and Wine Festival, where they won accolades for their innovative and delicious creations.

2017

In 2017, the bakery underwent a major renovation to accommodate its growing customer base. The new design featured an upgraded kitchen concept and a larger amount of tables to sit at and display food on. They also introduced gluten-free and vegan options, ensuring that everyone in the community could enjoy their baked goods. The bakery’s popularity continued to soar, and they were featured in several local food magazines.

2020

The global pandemic brought challenges, but the Golden Crust Bakery adapted by launching an online ordering system and offering home delivery services. They also started sharing recipes and baking tips to keep their engaged and connected during lockdowns.

2021

As restrictions eased, They introduced a new range of seasonal products, including holiday-themed cakes and pastries, which quickly became a hit. The year ended on a high note with the bakery being awarded the “Best Bakery in Gisborne” by a local food critic.

2023

In 2023, the Golden Crust Bakery continued to thrive. They launched a catering service, providing delicious baked goods for events and celebrations. The bakery’s commitment to quality and community remained unwavering, and they continued to be a cherished part of Gisborne’s culinary scene.

Our Story

The Golden Crust Bakery was born out of a shared dream between Sarah and Tom, a couple who had always been passionate about baking. Sarah, a talented pastry chef, and Tom, a skilled bread maker, met while working at a renowned bakery in Wellington. They often talked about opening their own bakery, where they could combine their skills and create a place that felt like home to their customers.

In 2012, they decided to take the plunge and moved to Gisborne, a town known for its close-knit community and appreciation for local produce. With their savings and a small loan, they found a charming spot in the heart of the town and began transforming it into the Golden Crust Bakery.

From the very beginning, Sarah and Tom were committed to using the finest, locally sourced ingredients. They spent countless hours perfecting their recipes, ensuring that every loaf of bread and pastry was made with love and care. Their dedication paid off as the bakery quickly became a beloved spot in Gisborne. Locals were drawn to the warm, inviting atmosphere and the delicious aroma of freshly baked goods. Over the years, the Golden Crust Bakery grew, but Sarah and Tom never lost sight of their original vision: to create a bakery that felt like a second home to their customers, where everyone could enjoy the simple pleasure of a well-made loaf of bread or a perfectly baked pastry.
